## What is going on in Big Podcast?

In every episode, Tim and Kyle talk about trends within the Podcast Industrial Complex (PIC). Then, at the end of each year, we go back through our list and see how we did.

Most of these trends (i.e. predictions) happen to come true.

However, to keep track of our success, we've compiled a list of all our predictions into a list for you to judge for yourself!

### Predictions for 2020

1. More True Crime podcasters will form content networks to improve their credibility and audience sizes in 2020 (<a href="./ep008-how-true-is-true-in-true-crime-podcasting">Episode #8</a>).
2. A podcaster will have media credentials big enough to ask the President of the United States a question in 2020 (<a href="./ep008-how-true-is-true-in-true-crime-podcasting">Episode #8</a>).
3. Google Podcasts will eventually be native to Android, will be on every phone, and become a “must-have” app for podcast listeners within the next three (3) years (<a href="./ep009-is-google-podcasts-too-late-or-just-in-time">Episode #9</a>).
4. We will make it to the 50 episodes this year and we will make another 50 next year (<a href="./ep010-overnight-success-and-other-podcasting-myths">Episode #10</a>).
5. We are going to see a lot more people do that one-and-done episode. But that’s ok because they are going to learn to appreciate what goes into producing a podcast and they will become sponsors of podcasters who they like (<a href="./ep010-overnight-success-and-other-podcasting-myths">Episode #10</a>). #5.1 - The next Anchor-like company will appear in 2020!

7. More podcasters will discover that recording before their audience live is one of the primary ways to grow their podcast 10-fold in 2019 (<a href="./ep012-is-it-live-or-is-it-podwrecked">Episode #12</a>).

### Predictions for 2019

1. We believe that we will reach Podwrecked Episode #50 before 2020 (by EOD on December 31st, 2019) (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
2. We think that more low-budget and/or free podcasting media hosting companies will go out of business in 2019 and that the entire free hosting space business model will come to an end (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
3. We do think Google will double down on Audio Indexing in 2019, making audio content like podcasts searchable for the first time. It won't be pretty, but it will get better in time and become part of their overall search tool (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
4. We don't think Google will start caring about podcasting to the point that will threaten Apple's market share in 2019 (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
5. We think there will be some new directory offerings that will start to threaten Apple's podcast directory market share in 2019 (<a href="./ep007-hiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
6. We don't think Apple will start caring about podcasting again, at least enough to fix the poor search experience built into iTunes in 2019 (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
7. We think that there will be an explosion of political podcasts that will be established to produce content about the Presidential Primaries in 2019, with a few surviving to become the go-to podcasts for the 2020 Presidential Election (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
8. We think there will be so many short-form podcasts that new Short-Form Podcast Directories will start popping up for those that like bite-sized podcasts for their daily fix or smart speaker briefings (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
9. We think Big News (e.g. CBS, NBC, ABC, FOX, CNN, BBC) will go all in with daily and weekly podcasts in 2019. We think there will be so many daily news podcasts that we will be overwhelmed by all the content (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
10. We think there will be an explosion of short-form podcasts (i.e. daily podcasts) that will take advantage of smart speakers like Amazon Alexa and Flash Briefings (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
11. We think that the podcast technology-feeding frenzy has yet to start. We don't think big companies will start buying little podcasting content companies to help them jump into the podcasting space just yet. It will be quiet in 2019... but maybe not for 2020 (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
12. Every Podcasting Host that charges money will be IAB certified in 2019. It will be the new standard for all upcoming podcast hosts that want to enter the industry (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
13. Podcasting will not go mainstream in 2019 but it will continue to develop -- there are just too many other distractions that grab attention to allow podcasting "mainstream" -- because 1. delivery, 2. people who know how or what podcasting is, 3. newbies getting into podcasting. Measurement is 75% of people know what a podcast is and knows what a podcast is. Listen once a month (<a href="./ep007-shiny-shiny-crystal-ball-of-podcasting">Episode #7</a>).
